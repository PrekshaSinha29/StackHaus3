About StackHaus

StackHaus began as a small experiment between architects and furniture designers who shared a simple frustration: most bookshelves are rigid, oversized, and rarely fit the spaces people actually live in. Apartments change, collections grow, and interiors evolve—but traditional furniture rarely adapts.

What started as a few modular prototypes in a workshop gradually became a system. By combining digital design tools with modular fabrication, the team developed a way to configure shelving that can expand, contract, and reorganize as needed. The idea was straightforward: furniture should behave more like architecture—structured, adaptable, and responsive to space.

Today, StackHaus focuses on creating configurable shelving systems that balance precision with simplicity. Each design begins digitally but is built with durable materials and straightforward assembly. The result is a set of modular elements that can be rearranged as homes, studios, and collections change over time.

For us, the goal has always been the same: make shelves that grow with the life around them.
